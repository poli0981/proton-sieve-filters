## ⚠️ Media Literacy & Source Verification

### Reliable News Indicators
- **Established Publications**: Long history of journalism
- **Professional Standards**: Editorial oversight and fact-checking
- **Transparency**: Clear authorship and source attribution
- **Corrections**: Willingness to correct errors publicly

### Red Flags to Watch
- **Sensational Headlines**: Excessive emotional language
- **Anonymous Sources**: Lack of attribution or verification
- **Biased Language**: Heavily partisan or inflammatory tone
- **Missing Context**: Isolated facts without broader picture

### Verification Best Practices
1. **Cross-Reference**: Check multiple reputable sources
2. **Primary Sources**: Look for original documents or statements
3. **Fact-Checkers**: Consult established fact-checking organizations
4. **Timeline**: Verify when information was published or updated
5. **Author Credentials**: Research journalist background and expertise
