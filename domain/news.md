## 🔒 Media Credibility & Verification

### High Credibility Sources
✅ **Established Publications:**
- Major newspapers with long journalism history
- Public broadcasting organizations
- Academic and scientific institutions
- Government health and safety agencies

### Verification Guidelines
1. **Cross-Reference**: Check multiple reputable sources
2. **Author Credentials**: Verify journalist background
3. **Publication Date**: Check for current information
4. **Source Attribution**: Look for proper citations
5. **Editorial Standards**: Consider publication's fact-checking process

### Red Flags to Avoid
⚠️ **Questionable Sources:**
```
- Websites with excessive pop-ups or ads
- Sources demanding immediate action
- Publications with obvious political bias without disclosure
- Sites mimicking legitimate news domain names
- Sources without clear authorship or contact information
```

## ⚙️ Implementation Guide

### Priority Configuration
1. **Essential Sources**: Major news outlets you trust and read regularly
2. **Specialized Interests**: Industry or topic-specific publications
3. **Local Coverage**: Regional newspapers and local news stations
4. **International Perspective**: Global news sources for broader context

### Retention Strategy
- **Breaking News**: 1 day (immediate action)
- **Daily News**: 2-3 days (reference value)
- **Analysis**: 7 days (deeper understanding)
- **Research**: 14+ days (long-term reference)

### Customization Options
- **Language Preferences**: Add non-English news sources
- **Regional Focus**: Include local and regional publications
- **Topic Interests**: Emphasize preferred subject areas
- **Credibility Standards**: Filter based on publication reputation

### Maintenance Schedule
- **Weekly**: Review new subscriptions and sources
- **Monthly**: Clean up inactive or changed domains
- **Quarterly**: Audit source credibility and relevance
- **Annually**: Complete review of news consumption habits
